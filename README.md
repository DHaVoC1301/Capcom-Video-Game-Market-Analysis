# Capcom-Video-Game-Market-Analysis
A multi-page Power BI &amp; Excel market intelligence dashboard analyzing Capcom's Resident Evil franchise performance on the PS5. Evaluates sales volume, development pipelines, and player sentiment alignment to deliver data-driven executive product roadmap strategies.

# Case Study: Maximizing Survival Horror Profitability – Capcom Product Strategy Analysis
By: DAMIANOOI | Data Analyst Portfolio Project (Power BI & Excel)

## 📌 1. Strategic Business Context & Problem
In the highly competitive gaming industry, a publisher like Capcom must decide how to allocate development budgets between two distinct production paths: developing brand-new, first-person modern mainline entries (e.g., RE7, Village) or investing in nostalgic, third-person remakes of classic titles (e.g., RE2, RE4). 

Using an operational dataset modeling product lifecycles across standard, deluxe, and value editions playable on the PlayStation 5, this analysis evaluates critical reception, consumer alignment, pricing structures, and gameplay length to deliver a data-driven product roadmap for future releases.

## 🛠️ 2. Tech Stack & Methodology
* **Analysis Engine:** Microsoft Excel (Advanced Data Architecture & Validation)
* **Visualization Engine:** Microsoft Power BI Desktop (Three-Page Interactive Layout)
* **Interactivity:** Dynamic Category Button Slicers & Timeline Slider Control

## 📊 3. Key Business Insights
Through cross-filtered analysis inside Power BI, the following core metrics were uncovered from the dataset:

* **The Absolute Revenue King:** **Resident Evil 7: Biohazard** remains the franchise's ultimate long-term sales driver, commanding the single highest sales performance bar in the dataset. First-person psychological horror represents a highly lucrative creative avenue for long-term tail revenue.
* **Production Pipeline Consistency:** When filtering down specifically to **Reimagined Classics (Remakes)**, Capcom demonstrates masterclass development consistency. Across all remake editions, the average core gameplay length trends at a highly uniform **10.5 hours**. 
* **The Remake Performance Premium:** Despite relying on older story frameworks, third-person remakes achieve an exceptional average Metacritic rating of **91-93**, which directly cushions them against early post-launch retail price decay compared to lower-rated iterations.

## 💡 4. Data-Driven Executive Recommendations
Based on the dashboard metrics, I recommend that Capcom implement the following three product choices for future PS5 and next-gen pipelines:

1. **Maintain the Hybrid Release Loop:** Capcom should not choose one format over the other. The optimal financial strategy is a strict **staggered alternation loop**: launch a massive first-person mainline entry (like RE7 or Resident Evil Requiem) to push technological boundaries, followed two years later by a nostalgia-backed third-person remake (like a Resident Evil 5 Remake) to generate reliable, high-margin revenue.
2. **Optimize the 10.5-Hour Development Sweet Spot:** Production teams should design remakes to strictly target the identified **10.5-hour engagement ceiling**. The data shows that adding extra game length does not scale up sales proportionally; keeping remakes compact reduces internal development costs while maximizing critical acclaim.
3. **Protect Value with Deluxe Sequencing:** To combat eventual sales stagnation of older mainline titles, Capcom should push "Gold" or "Deluxe" bundles at a **$49.99 pricing anchor** rather than aggressively slashing base game prices to $19.99 too early. The data indicates bundle editions successfully capture premium secondary buyers who value comprehensive content packages over raw discounts.
